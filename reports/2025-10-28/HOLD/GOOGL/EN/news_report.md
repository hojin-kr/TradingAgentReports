Macro backdrop (relevant to GOOG and peers)
- Inflation and rates: With inflation showing progress, rate expectations remain supportive for high-growth tech equities. Equities have benefited from a relatively stable macro regime: modest rate cuts are possible if growth remains robust, but policy risk remains a flywheel risk if inflation re-accelerates.
- Global demand and corporate IT budgets: The enterprise IT spend cycle remains a key driver for cloud vendors. Large enterprises continue to prioritize AI-enabled cloud services and data analytics, which benefits Alphabet Cloud alongside AWS and Azure.
- Advertising cycle: Alphabet’s core ad business (Search and YouTube) tracks macro ad demand and brand advertising spend. In a resilient macro, ad budgets tend to hold up better than broad-economy capex, making Alphabet relatively defensively exposed to ad-cycle dynamics but with upside if AI-enhanced targeting and product placements boost monetization.
- AI/Cloud capex tilt: The AI/cloud upgrade cycle remains the dominant secular theme. Incremental AI features, data center efficiency, and processor/accelerator ecosystems (Google’s own chips and partnerships) can lift cloud margins and future revenue growth.

- Regulation and policy: U.S./EU scrutiny on big-tech practices, data privacy rules, and antitrust considerations remain external risks that could affect investor sentiment and strategic options.
