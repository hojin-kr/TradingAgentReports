Baseline framework (adjustable to your numbers)
1) Hedge construct (start point)
- Core hedge: collar on QQQ
  - Long: QQQ puts roughly 6–9% OTM, expiration 3–6 months out (provides a built-in floor).
  - Short: QQQ calls roughly 0–4% above the current price, same expiration (finances part of the hedge and preserves some upside).
  - Net: reduce net QQQ exposure by about 20–30% relative to a full long allocation (hedge expense offset by short call premium).

- Alternative (cost/dynamics sake): protective puts only
  - If collars are too costly or execution is tight, use protective puts at 5–7% OTM with 3–9 months horizon (no upside cap).

2) Net exposure and capital allocation
- Target net long exposure after hedges: roughly 70–85% of a full long position in QQQ (i.e., 15–30% hedged/cushioned). If risk conditions worsen, move toward the lower end (70% net long) and if conditions improve, gradually re-tilt toward the 85% corridor.
- Ballast ballast: maintain 5–15% of portfolio in cash or short-duration Treasuries to dampen drawdowns and maintain optionality for re-entry.

3) Risk triggers and dynamic plan
- Clearly define reallocation rules:
  - If QQQ breaks below a defined support and volatility regime worsens for a sustained period, intensify hedges (add puts, extend duration) and/or push more into cash.
  - If macro data improves, rate expectations stabilize, or AI monetization momentum strengthens with margin expansion, unwind hedges in steps (e.g., 25% at a time) toward target exposure.
- Roll cadence: consider a ladder of expiries (3, 6, 9, 12 months) to avoid a single roll decision in a stress moment and to smooth carry costs.

4) Diversification and protection beyond QQQ
- Diversify hedges to reduce mega-cap idiosyncratic risk:
  - Add a small sleeve of SPY puts or broad-market hedges to reduce single-name shock.
  Add a light volatility hedge (e.g., defined-VIX strategy) to diversify the protection mix.

  - If data feeds fail, rely on price/volatility triggers and a pre-cleared roll schedule.

6) Review and learn
